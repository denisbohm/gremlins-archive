As you can see there was a tie in the Girls genre. There were plenty of votes in the other categories, but there were not 5 or more more for any specific kit. The voters were a diverse bunch!

We were not trying to find "the best" kit for everyone, is there any such thing? But we did ask everyone to vote for their personal most favorite kits. As such, the Gremmies recognize the most popular personal favorite kits. Since the Gremmies were decided by a mass voting I think that is the most appropriate way to describe the award. Thanks for telling us what your favorite kits are!

To be eligable a kit must have been released sometime in 1996 or late 1995. One kit in each category that has the most votes won (a kit must have had at least five votes to be a winner). People were allowed to vote for as many categories as they liked and up to two kits in each category. The voting ended December 31st. Each company that produced a winning kit will receive a 1996 Gremmie award!

Photo of the Gremmie award will appear here soon...

Some things taken into consideration when deciding on favorite kits were:

  • Originality - character, overall design
  • Sculpting - detail, likeness, pose
  • Base - provides good support for the figure, enhances the look
  • Scale - shows detail, dispay are needed
  • Casting - offset seams, bubbles
  • Material - easy to work with, robust
  • Assembly - part fit, hidden seams, alignment, instructions
  • Packaging - attractive box art, well protected, size
  • Availability - hobby stores, mail order, direct
  • Price - appropriate
