Chiller October 1996: Review

Review by:
Ed Bowkley (ebowkley@webspan.net)

Photos by:
Michael Oprean (opream@msn.com)
Ed Bowkley (ebowkley@webspan.net)

eems like only yesterday that I attended my first Chiller Halloween Expo. Actually it's been a little over 2 years that I was introduced to the "MADNESS AT THE MEADOWLANDS" and now being a card carrying member of the meadowlands mafia, let me tell you, the ride just keeps getting better and better! There are so many twists and turns that this show HAS to be 3 days long just so you can look at everything!! That's how much there is to it! Over 300 dealers tables, guests galore, a costume ball with live music, model contest and last but not least KITS!! Resin, vinyl, plastic, new, vintage and rare kits from all over the globe! That's right the GLOBE!! Garage kit companies from Japan, England, Canada, Sweden and all points of the U.S.A. make the trek to Chiller! And why not. With close to 10,000 people in attendance, this is the place to be and be seen!!

I really don't know where to start,so how about with some of the kits that caught my eye! First off Swede Creations had a Death Dealer kit that I must have in my collection at any cost. The kit was sculpted by Joke Lindman and is a must for any Frazetta fan!! 3D from canada's new Namor (sub-mariner) is true to the Bill Evert version and calls out to be displayed in any comic collector's hall of fame. Python Press amazed everyone with their WALKING Centaur Cyclops! (THAT'S RIGHT THE THING WALKS!!) If your into Harryhausen kits get it quick because the kit is limited to only 12 worldwide! Alternative Images new Darkness kit and Conan Kits are real Eye catchers! AI comes out with so many new kits each and every show that it's hard to keep up with them! Good job guys! Needful things new Braveheart (Mel gibson) and Nightlife Productions new Fantasy line drew raves from guys and gals alike! One kit I MUST get (because my wife went nuts for it) is a Fantastic Genie, ala I Dream of.... Sculpted by Joy and Tom Snyder of Florida. If you want to get in your better halves good graces, BUY THIS KIT!! That will keep you safe for many kits to come. I got a chance to see the Barb Wire Kit that Dave Fisher had at his table, great build up! Susumu Sugita and Fewture were turning heads with kits that are now part of my ever growing collection, namely Gullotina and Blood Hammer along with Operation In Progress!! GEOmetric, Mental Mischief, Mad Labs, Screamin', Resinator, Death Inc all had great kits on display. Fairbanx Models new Madame Kwasimoto and Lily Munster sold out before I could get my greedy paws on them! Remember this is just the tip of the Iceberg here. You had Tom Savini at the Jayco table with their new Fluffy kit sculpted by Tom, Wolf's Den, Fat Mike's.... Youch! This is supposed to be a show review not a who's who of the figure kit industry!! The magazines were on hand with Jerry Buchanan and Tom Ellis representing The Modelers Resource; Gordy Dutt, Rich Larson and myself with Kitbuilders; Dave and Cindy Fisher along with Terry Webb from Amazing Figure Modeler; The Chiller Theatre staff; Harris Comics (Vampirella) and Ted Boghus from SPFX as well as Scarlett Street!

Don't go yet cause it's time to introduce some of the guests that were on hand. Like Jeff Franco, Lina Romay, Doug (Pinhead) Bradley, Russ Meyers, Anne Francis, Candy Clark, James Hong, Tom Savini, Yuko Moriyama who played Iria in the live action Zeram movies, those ever popular Scream Queens as well as those Lovelies from Troma (Hi Tiffany) making Grown men Drool like Teenagers, John Russo and Bill Hinzman my Zombie friends from Night Of The Living Dead and one of my personal Faves Sara Karloff. Had enough.. well read on!!

From what I was told the Costume Ball was a Blast with Dead Elvis and the Screamin' Barbies Rocking the house and the costumes are getting so wild nowadays that I can't describe them here! But Trust me you gotta see it to believe it! The reason I wasn't at the ball was that a small group of 23 gremlins and dealers opted for a steak dinner at a restaurant that I frequent. Let's just say that the conversation ran the gambit.. until the food arrived and then you could of heard a pin drop! Plans are underway for next years dinner and I think they'll take my reservations next time!! With everyone there wearing those wristbands at dinner they must of thought that the asylum let us all out on weekend passes!! Good food, great people and a fantastic show! It doesn't get any better than this!!

Nope not done yet! Let me tell you a little about the model contest. With over 200 kits entered this one is a hard nut to crack! In my humble opinion it has to be one of the hardest to judge bar none! I pity the judges here because the fans really go the extra mile to impress and you really have to or be lost in a sea of resin! John Bushlow from Nightlife held his annual blank customizing contest. You know once this one catches on the big boys are really going to sit up and take notice! This is not a easy task to create a masterpiece out of a blank and although only about 10-12 kits were entered it was pretty tough to judge! They all looked good to me and I was one of the judges!! All I can say is if this one gets any bigger, John get yourself a new judge!! No way I could pick a hands down winner (I can't even make one of those blanks look near as good if I tried!!)

Don't worry the end is almost near!! Retailers from all over were also on hand selling kits. Paul Antonelli's Character Shop still selling kits at Crazy Eddie Prices! Puff-n-Stuff, Kitboy, Chiller Theatre store, Image Trading and countless more, too many to name. Listen, I can't go on.. Let's just say that the entire resin, vinyl and styrene kit world was here in one form or another, because I'm running out of ink and I didn't even get to the pictures yet! You know I had a chance to meet gremlins that I now consider good friends, I'm dead broke from buying kits that I couldn't resist and I hope each and every gremlin out there gets a chance to make the trip to my stomping grounds and go broke with me at next year's April or October show! I'll save a seat for you at the restaurant!!
