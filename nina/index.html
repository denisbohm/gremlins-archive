The Fever

How can you be around garage kits and not catch the fever! You can't! And what's the next step when some resin heads get to shooting the breeze? Produce a kit! That's just what happened...

The Concept

Since we meet through Gremlins in the Garage, it was a natural to do some kind of gremlin kit first. The first idea was to do a gremlin standing at a table sculpting all the elements that can be found in the GitG navigation bar. Spice it up by having the gremlin croutching on the bench and it would make a neat kit. Of course, then someone has a blast of inspiration - Nina Gremlin Skin! Thanks, Leann! Now we have some great elements: beautiful woman, gremlins, water. What more could anyone ask for!

The Pose

Nina wearing a gremlin suit. Her arms straight out to the sides and her head arched back. Ankle to knee deep in boiling frothing water. The water is contained inside a fountain carved out of stone with gargoyle faces, etc. New born gremlin pods are popping out of her skin. Lights will be riged up in the "water" to produce a weird flashing effect in the water of the gremlins reproducing. A couple partially formed gremlins stretching to escape their pods. A couple fully formed gremlins grining at Nina. An expression that is a mixture of pain and pleasure as she's birthing the little fellas. Sorry, I've gotta go hose myself down!

The Rules

Before we go any further, what are the rules of this collaborative game? Keep good notes, take lots of progress pics, we're going to want to write this project up as an article for gremlins.

The Date

If we work slowly and steadily on the project, we should be able to have something done by next March. It'd be a blast to be able to present copies of the kit to Nirasawa and Chris Walas! Let's avoid a last minute crunch.

The Plan

  1. Come up with a cool pose that we all like.
  2. Sculpt a nude (oh, my) woman in the pose. She need not be too detailed. Most of the body will have a scaly suit sculpted over it. Do we want part of her breasts exposed? Maybe just the edges of the nipples? Traditionally, Nina is clothed, albeit scantily. Her face, throat, and upper torso should be fairly detailed. 1:6 scale.
  3. Cast a couple of copies to work on.
  4. Add the gremlin suit and Nina's artifical arm.
  5. Create the base. We should bring some other people in on this - specifically to work on sculpting the little gremlins.
  6. Make molds and cast a couple of prototypes.
  7. Paint a prototype.
