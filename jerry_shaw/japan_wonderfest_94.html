Japan Wonderfest 94: Photo Tour

Review by Jerry Shaw
(jshaw@primenet.com)
attended the Japanese Modeling Convention Wonder Festival while I was in Tokyo, Japan on August 21st of 1994. I took about 200 pictures of the models there. Here is a quick writeup of the convention, and an index of the pictures I took there.

Wonder Festival, Summer 94
Sunday, August 21, 1994
Tokyo, Chuo Ku, Harumi
 (Just outside Tokyo, Japan, Southeast of the Ginza district)

The show started at 10:30 am and closed at 5:00 pm. The admission price was 1500 yen (about $16) which includes a 96 page Guide Book. There were about 485 dealers listed in the Guide book, with 85 listed as Pro Dealers and 400 listed as Amateur Dealers. About two thirds of the dealers were model kit dealers, but about a third were toy dealers.

I would guess that over 50 percent of the figure kit dealers were small, independent dealers, with about the same percent of the models being non-pro models. As such, the kits may not be available outside Japan. Many of the models were show specials, available only at the show, and are no longer available. A lot of the models were original designs, not based on any other character designs.

You may notice that a lot of the pictures are of girl figures. This accurately represents the percentage of female figure kits at the convention. Over 70 percent of the models at the convention were of females, with a low percent of robot models and monsters, and an even smaller percent of male figures of any type.

I only made one pass through the dealer's room with my camera, and missed a lot of the models. I would say that I only got pictures of about 50 percent of the models there, maybe even less. In all, I got pictures of over 100 models.

Many of the kits I photographed were original models, done by independent amateur modelers. So, there are a lot of the pictures that neither my friends nor I could identify. Also, there were so many small dealers that we could not even identify the maker of a lot of the kits. I wrote as much information as I was able to find on each photo. In the following list, I used a figure of $1.00 to 100 yen for the prices.

Here are some technical details of how the pictures were produced. The pictures were taken with a Cannon A1 SLR camera with a Vivitar 35-105MM 1:3.5 Zoom Lens with Macro, and a Vivitar 285 flash with inflatable 6"x8" Flash Soft Box diffuser on the front. The flash unit was mounted on the hotshoe on the top of the camera during shooting. The film used was Kodak Royal Gold 400 speed print film. Through experimentation on several rolls of film, it was determined that the best depth of field tradeoff was obtained with an aperture of F8 or F11, with the flash unit set to a manual setting of 1/16 power. I took two pictures of each model, using both apertures. The film was processed and transferred to Photo-CD pictures through my local photo dealer, using Kodalux processing labs. The cost was $3 per roll for processing and $0.79 per image transferred. This resulted in 151 images (4 rolls of film) on one Photo CD (number 0416) and 36 images on a second Photo CD (number 0634).

All the files are JPEG files converted from the Photo CD files. They were cropped and reduced from the original Photo CD resolution of 2048x3072 to fit on a 640x480 screen. Those pictures with any adult content at all are labeled ADULT.
